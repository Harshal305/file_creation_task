India, officially the Republic of India,[j][20] is a country in South Asia.
 It is the seventh-largest country by area; the most populous country since 2023;[21] and since its independence in 1947,
 the world's most populous democracy.[22][23][24] Bounded by the Indian Ocean on the south, the Arabian Sea on the southwest,
 and the Bay of Bengal on the southeast, it shares land borders with Pakistan to the west;[k] China, Nepal, and Bhutan to the north; and 
 Bangladesh and Myanmar to the east.
 In the Indian Ocean, India is near Sri Lanka and the Maldives; its Andaman and Nicobar
 Islands share a maritime border with Thailand, Myanmar, and Indonesia.
India has been a federal republic since 1950, governed through a democratic parliamentary system.
 It is a pluralistic, multilingual and multi-ethnic society.
 India's population grew from 361 million in 1951 to over 1.4 billion in 2023.[63] During this time,
 its nominal per capita income increased from US$64 annually to US$2,601, and its literacy rate from 16.6% to 74%.
